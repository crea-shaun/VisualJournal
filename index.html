Feb 2026 Thoughts

Why I Shoot Film

There's something about the wait. The not-knowing. Digital gives you everything instantly...

There's something about the wait. The not-knowing. Digital gives you everything instantly ‚Äî and maybe that's the problem. Film forces you to be intentional. Every frame costs money. Every click of the shutter is a commitment.

I started shooting film because I wanted to slow down. I was burning through 500 photos a weekend on my phone and keeping maybe 3. With film, I shoot 36 frames on a roll and keep 30. The ratio flipped.

It's not about nostalgia. It's about discipline. And honestly? The grain, the color, the imperfections ‚Äî they feel more real than anything a sensor produces.

Jan 2026 Process

Car Shows & Found Moments

Most people go to car shows for the cars. I go for everything around them...

Most people go to car shows for the cars. I go for everything around them. The rust patterns on a 60-year-old fender. The way light hits chrome at golden hour. The old man who's been polishing the same Buick for 40 years.

Photography taught me to see details. Car shows are a goldmine for that ‚Äî texture, color, character. Every car has a story, and you can read it in the paint chips and patina.

My favorite shots from shows are never the hero angles. They're the close-ups. The overlooked stuff. That's where the real beauty is.

Dec 2025 Life

Shooting Family

The cookout photos are my favorite ones I've ever taken...

The cookout photos are my favorite ones I've ever taken. Not because they're technically perfect ‚Äî they're not. The exposure is off on a few. Some are slightly out of focus.

But they're real. That's my family, in their element, not posing for anything. The food trailer, the folding chairs, the bounce house in the background. That's a Saturday.

I think the best photography isn't about making things look beautiful. It's about showing things as they are and letting the beauty speak for itself.

Nov 2025 Gear

First Roll

I remember picking up my first developed roll. Hands shaking a little...

I remember picking up my first developed roll. Hands shaking a little. Not because I was nervous ‚Äî because I genuinely didn't know what I'd captured.

Half the roll was overexposed. A few frames were completely black. But there were maybe 5 or 6 shots that made me stop scrolling through the scans and just stare.

That feeling ‚Äî that surprise ‚Äî is what got me hooked. You can't replicate it with digital. The delay between capture and reveal is where the magic lives.
